Reduce

Reduce: verb - Make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Ways to Reduces

  • Bring your own bags when shopping.
  • Buy used items. Used items are usually cheaper than new and still just as good. You can purchase clothing, building supplies and even electronics used.
  • Use reusable items instead of disposable. Use clothing napkins at home. Bring your own silverware and cups to work and school.
  • Buy items with less packaging.
  • Get a water filter and a reusable water bottle instead of buying water all the time.
  • Take care of the things you own. If you take care of things you own you won't have to replace them as often.
  • Borrow instead of buying. You can get most book and DVDs at your local library. If you only use powertools and lawn epuipment a few times a year, borrow them. Many cities now have tool share programs.
  • Donate. All sorts of things can be donated. Clothes, books, tools, furniture, electronics, even building supplies can be donated. You may even be able to get a tax deduction for your donation.

All of these ideas help to reduce the amount of waste created and the amount of waste that ends up in landfills.
